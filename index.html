Enchiladas Rojas

History of Enchiladas

Enchiladas are a common traditional food from the country of Mexico. Enchiladas originate back to the Mayans. Corn was a very abundant and popular crop at the time with corn tortillas as common as corn. Originally, tortillas were called Tlaxcalli, but when Spanish conquistadors arrived, they renamed it to the now known tortillas. One day the Mayans rolled fish with tortillas and due to this, they brought and made the first kind of enchiladas which would also be adopted by Aztecs. Overtime, enchiladas became the stuffed tortillas with various meats and cheeses and different sauces to become the enchiladas we know today.

One Recipe for Enchiladas Rojas

One type of Enchiladas that can be made are Enchiladas Rojas. Traditionally, Enchiladas Rojas are made with guajillo chillies which is what is used to make the red sauce seen on these Enchiladas. Tortillas are then dipped into the sauce and then rolled and from there enchiladas can be filled with chicken, cheese, or refried beans. What you choose is mainly preference but here is one recipe for Enchiladas Rojas.

  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 3 chicken breasts, cooked and meat shredded
  • 6 dried guajillo chillies
  • 4 tomatoes
  • 1 small onion, peeled
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 stemed fresh serrano or jalapeño chilli
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • 1/3 tsp dried oregano
  • A pinch freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 corn tortilla baked and hardened
  • 1 1/2 cups rapeseed oil
  • Salt to taste

Optional

  • 3/4 cup crumbled Halumi or Feta
  • Sliced white onion rings
  • Avocado slices
  • Sour cream

Instructions

  1. Remove the stems and seeds from the chillies
  2. Heat a frying pan over high heat and toast the dried guajillo chillies, making sure they do not burn
  3. Put the chillies in a bowl and cover with 2 cups of boiling water; leave aside until chillies are soft, about 10 minutes. Drain them, keeping the liquid, put them in a blender with 1/2 cup of the soaking water. Blend until smooth and keep salsa aside.
  4. Charred the tomatoes, onion, garlic and fresh chillies until all the vegetables look very charred. Leave aside to cool.
  5. Fry the chilli purée in a frying pan adding one tbsp oil, stir constantly until getting a thick consistency
  6. Put the salsa into the blender again together with the vegetables, stock, oregano, baked tortilla, pepper and blend until smooth. Pour through a strainer into a sauce pan and return back to the heat, bring to a boil, and then reduce heat to medium-low, until the salsa has reduce
  7. Add salt to taste and keep it warm
  8. Heat the rest of the oil in a frying pan over medium heat
  9. Using a pair of tongs fry for around 1 min the tortillas in the oil one by one
  10. Then one by one introduce each tortilla into the warm chilli salsa, coating the tortilla then take it out, add some shredded chicken, roll and continue doing this with each tortilla until they are all done
  11. Serve them all in a large plate, scatter some cheese over the top, ring onions and if you want add some avocado slices and sour cream
